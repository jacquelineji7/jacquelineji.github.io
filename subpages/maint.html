What is the problem? (Prompt)

Improve the upkeep of campus facilities by creating a new system for reporting any facilities that may need maintenance or repair. Design an experience that allows students to report building or equipment issues on campus. Consider the process of those filing the report and of those receiving and taking action on the issues.

How does the current system work? (Research)

I decided to approach the problem by really understanding it first. I spoke with people at the service desk, student union, library receptions, etc. Turn out, what I heard the most is:

“I’m not exactly sure how it works, but you can probably talk to …”

If even the staff does not know the process, it would only be harder for the students.

Who are the people involved ?

Research on the students

“I never bother reporting it because I assume it would eventually be fixed.” - Kelly (4th year AFM student)

“I don’t really know how to report it, there are usually other chairs that work anyway.” - Han (2nd year CS student)

“Mostly just lazy, it is too much work.” - Jenny (3rd year Math student)

Problems I indentified after my research

  • 1. Too much paperwork
  • 2. Unsure which department to reach
  • 3. No staff around / not business hour
  • 4. Too much work describing locations and the issues
  • 5. Methods of reporting can only be found through googling

How can these problems be solved?

  • How might we make the report process clear with least actions required?
  • How might we ensure all reports are being received and handled properly?
  • How might we reduce the paperwork without losing the management quality?

Design decisions

Why chose yellow as the primary colour?

  • Yellow and black are the two colours that represent University of Waterloo.
  • Choosing such colour palette help build
    - Sense of familiarity
    - Trust between the platform and users
  • I have also used material design as a guide to choose the shade of yellow

Changes I made from wireframes to prototypes

It was a tough decision to make getting rid of the wheel and placing the camera button on the left side of the screen.

The left interface seems cooler, I figured the mockup on the right is more intuitive and and easier to navigate.

To be improved

If I have more time, I would try to improve the following things:

  • - the flow of scheduling and assigning to worker
  • - The detailed data visualization of the statistics page
  • - A case study on where the QR codes should be placed (E.g Line-ups, study table, etc)
  • - Improve the UI as I ended up spending most time doing the research
